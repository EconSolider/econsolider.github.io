- `<strong>`Lu, Z.`</strong>`, Kameda, K., Deng, Y., Hua, P., Yin, R., Yabu, M., and Liu, S., 2026. Screen Time and Human Capital: Evidence from a Disaster-Driven School Smartphone Deregulation.
  **Abstract**: We estimate the causal effect of smartphone screen time on cognitive and non-cognitive human capital by exploiting a disaster-driven reversal of a long-standing school smartphone ban as an instrument for individual weekly screen time, using a 2015-2021 longitudinal panel of primary and lower secondary students. The deregulation raised weekly screen time by 1.43 hours. Each additional policy-induced hour lowers academic performance by 0.6 percent of the pre-policy mean and significantly reduces multiple non-cognitive dimensions measured, including autonomy, considerateness, cooperativeness, and optimism. The dominant channel is time reallocation rather than sleep disruption: one hour of screen time displaces 0.53 hours of self-study and explains 23 percent of the cognitive decline, while the sleep channel is statistically indistinguishable from zero. These results suggest that school smartphone bans are a low-cost policy for protecting human capital formation during adolescence.
